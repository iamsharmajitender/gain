## Design for AI Evaluation

<div className="gain-section-lead">
  Evaluation is how enterprise AI proves it is fit to ship: and fit to stay shipped. A one-off test
  suite is not enough. G.A.I.N Evaluation treats validation as a continuous pipeline aligned with
  business rules, instrumented on the request path, and backed by operational infrastructure.
</div>

## Evaluation pipeline

<div className="gain-section-lead">
  Evaluation is a <strong>pipeline</strong>, not a test. Scores are produced after the same stages a
  production request traverses: retrieval, generation, validation: so offline results predict
  online behavior.
</div>

Ask before you ship: **What defines “good” for this use case?** **Which layer catches which failure
mode?**

## G.A.I.N applied to evaluation

<Details className="gain-accordion" summary="G · Grounded: What defines “good”?">
  Grounded evaluation anchors scores in business rules and compliance: not generic benchmark
  leaderboards. “Good” is defined by policy, risk appetite, and domain accountability.

  **Components**

  - Policy correctness: outputs respect allowlists, blocklists, and entitlement rules
  - Compliance checks: regulatory, classification, and residency requirements pass before release
  - Risk thresholds: tiered gates for high-impact decisions (financial, medical, legal)
  - Golden criteria: use-case owners define pass/fail rubrics tied to business outcomes

  **Design questions**

  - Who signs off on what “good” means?
  - What score blocks a release or triggers escalation?

  **Principle:** Evaluation must align with business rules.
</Details>

<Details className="gain-accordion" summary="A · Adaptive: Evaluation as a pipeline">
  Adaptive evaluation runs continuously across offline, online, and human layers. Results feed
  rollbacks, prompt updates, and retrieval tuning: evaluation is how systems learn what broke.

  **Components**

  - Offline eval: regression on golden sets before every model, prompt, or index change
  - Online eval: production sampling, shadow scoring, and drift baselines
  - Human eval: expert review queues for edge cases, disputes, and calibration
  - Validator stage: schema, grounding, and business-rule gates in the scoring path

  **Design questions**

  - How often do offline runs gate deployment?
  - What online signal triggers a human review?

  **Principle:** Evaluation is a pipeline, not a test.
</Details>

<Details className="gain-accordion" summary="I · Intelligent: What is being evaluated?">
  Intelligent evaluation measures AI behavior end-to-end: not just final text. Relevance without
  correct reasoning, or fluent answers without proper tool use, still fail in production.

  **Components**

  - Relevance: did retrieval return the right context for the question?
  - Correctness: is the answer factually supported and policy-compliant?
  - Reasoning: are plan steps coherent; does the chain of thought hold?
  - Tool usage: correct tool selected, valid arguments, policy-respecting invocation

  **Design questions**

  - Are we scoring output only, or the full behavior trace?
  - How do we eval multi-step agent and RAG flows?

  **Principle:** Measure behavior, not just output.
</Details>

<Details className="gain-accordion" summary="N · Native: Evaluation infrastructure">
  Native evaluation needs platform infrastructure: datasets, replay, and harnesses are operational
  systems with SLAs, not spreadsheets on a shared drive.

  **Components**

  - Eval datasets: versioned golden questions, expected behaviors, and rubric metadata
  - Replay systems: reproduce production requests against new models or prompts deterministically
  - Test harnesses: CI integration, scheduled regression, and comparison across versions
  - Score stores: historical results, trend lines, and release gate audit trails

  **Design questions**

  - How are datasets versioned and owned?
  - Can we replay last week’s failures against today’s build?

  **Principle:** Evaluation needs operational infrastructure.
</Details>

## Key patterns

<Details className="gain-accordion" summary="Golden datasets per capability">
  Maintain separate golden sets for LLM, RAG, and agent use cases. A chat benchmark does not validate
  retrieval quality; a Q&A set does not validate tool orchestration.
</Details>

<Details className="gain-accordion" summary="Regression gates in CI/CD">
  Block promotion when offline scores drop below threshold on any critical dimension. Eval gates
  belong in the pipeline: not in a quarterly review.
</Details>

<Details className="gain-accordion" summary="LLM-as-judge (with guardrails)">
  Use models to score relevance and faithfulness at scale: but calibrate against human labels and
  never use a judge as the only compliance gate.
</Details>

<Details className="gain-accordion" summary="Citation and grounding checks">
  For RAG, score whether each claim is supported by a retrieved chunk. Grounding eval is the
  fastest path to measuring hallucination rate with business meaning.
</Details>

<Details className="gain-accordion" summary="Agent task success metrics">
  For agents, score end-to-end task completion, tool accuracy, policy violations, and steps to
  completion: not just whether the final message sounds right.
</Details>
