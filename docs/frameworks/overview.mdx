## The Core Principles

<div className="gain-section-lead">
AI systems are not intelligent components. They are governed systems operating inside enterprise constraints.
</div>

<Details className="gain-accordion" summary="Grounded, Truth, context and knowledge alignment">
  Grounded systems anchor every response in verified context. They connect models to knowledge bases, enforce citation and traceability, and align outputs with organizational truth: reducing hallucination and building trust in production AI.
</Details>

<Details className="gain-accordion" summary="Adaptive, Learning, feedback and continuous evolution">
  Adaptive systems learn from real-world usage. Feedback loops, evaluation pipelines, and human-in-the-loop workflows ensure models and agents improve over time rather than degrading silently in production.
</Details>

<Details className="gain-accordion" summary="Intelligent, Reasoning, agents and decision systems">
  Intelligent systems go beyond simple prompts. They orchestrate agents, apply reasoning chains, and make structured decisions: turning LLMs into reliable components within larger decision architectures.
</Details>

<Details className="gain-accordion" summary="Native, Scalable, modular and future-ready design">
  Native systems are built for the cloud from day one. Modular boundaries, observable pipelines, and platform-native patterns ensure AI capabilities scale with the business without becoming fragile monoliths.
</Details>

**Operating model**

- **[G.A.I.N AIOM](/frameworks/gain-aiom)**: teams, planes, and delivery boundaries

**Capability patterns**

- **G.A.I.N LLM**: model integration and production patterns
- **G.A.I.N RAG**: retrieval and knowledge alignment patterns
- **G.A.I.N Agents**: agent orchestration and governance
- **G.A.I.N MCP**: governed Model Context Protocol integration
- **G.A.I.N Observability**: telemetry, quality measurement, and audit trails
- **G.A.I.N Evaluation**: validation pipelines, scoring, and release gates
- **G.A.I.N Identity**: identity-aware context, entitlements, and access
